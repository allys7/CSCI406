{\bf
Practical Analysis
}
\end{center}

Assume array $A$ is indexed from $1$ to $n$.

\noindent
INEFFICIENT\_SORT($A$, $n$)\\
1. \For $i = 1$ \To $n!$ \Do\\
2. \indent Boolean {\em sortedSoFar} = TRUE;\\
3. \indent $j = 1$;\\
4. \indent $P$ = nextPermutation($A$); \\
5. \indent \While $j < n$ \And {\em sortedSoFar} \Do \\
6. \indent \indent \If $P[j] > P[j+1]$ \\
7. \indent \indent \Then {\em sortedSoFar} = FALSE\\
8. \indent \indent $j$++\\
9. \indent \If ({\em sortedSoFar}) \Then \Output $P$ \\

Analyze the worst-case complexity of INEFFICIENT\_SORT assuming that
the nextPermutation function always takes $\Theta(n)$ time.
